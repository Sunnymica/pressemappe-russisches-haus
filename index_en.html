The Russian House Berlin

An EU-sanctioned Russian agency operates a 29,000 m² cultural centre in the heart of Berlin – as if there were no sanctions and no war. One Berlin citizen holds the line.

What is this about?

  • Rossotrudnitschestwo – on the EU sanctions list since July 2022 – continues to operate the Russian House freely
  • The German government pays €70,000 per year in property tax from the federal budget
  • The Berlin public prosecutor's office is investigating possible sanctions violations
  • Henry Lindemeier has protested in front of the building daily since June 2024 – over 100 complaints filed against him, 1 unlawful arrest
  • Six major media outlets have covered the story: Berliner Morgenpost, ZEIT, TAZ, t-online, Süddeutsche Zeitung, Tages-Anzeiger (Switzerland)
Why now The Russian House is using German law to block Lindemeier's registered protest – by filing counter-demonstrations with police support. Active investigations by the public prosecutor and the data protection authority are ongoing.
